NHS hospital admissions, 1998/99 to 2023/24: burden and per-patient intensity by ICD-10 grouping

Tharun Gurunathan

This page goes with my written report. The main view is an interactive treemap of NHS hospital admissions for the last 26 years, and four supporting charts look at how the picture changes over time, where each diagnosis sits on a burden-versus-intensity plane, how the broader super-chapters compare, and a radial restatement of the same hierarchy. The numbers come from the Primary Diagnosis Summary sheet of the Admitted Patient Care series.

26 financial years (1998/99 - 2023/24) 4,135 rows after preprocessing 285 ICD-10 entries 20 chapters 10 super-chapters

1. About the dataset

The source is NHS Digital's Hospital Episode Statistics, Admitted Patient Care series. I combined 26 annual workbooks (1998/99 to 2023/24) into one table. The older years come as legacy .xls files, so I read those with python-calamine; the newer years are .xlsx and load with openpyxl. From each workbook I take the Primary Diagnosis Summary sheet, which means the analysis sits at the ICD-10 block-range level (e.g. A00-A09) with a few individual 3-character codes that the summary sheet lists on their own. The deeper 3-character and 4-character detail tabs were not used.

The three measures the charts rely on are derived during preprocessing:

StepResult
1Parse 26 Excel workbooks (legacy and modern schemas) Long table: code, year, finished episodes, admissions, bed-days, mean wait
2Attach ICD-10 chapter letter and chapter name 21 WHO chapters
3Custom super-chapter rollup 10 broader clinical groupings
4Compute MeanLOS = BedDays / FCE Average length of stay per finished episode
5Compute Burden = Admissions × MeanLOS Total bed-day demand for the diagnosis
6Compute Intensity = MeanWait × MeanLOS Per-patient time burden, used as the colour scale
7Fix the colour range to the 10th and 90th percentiles of Intensity across all years Comparable colour interpretation across years and charts

2. Treemap of burden by ICD-10 grouping

Every rectangle is one of the top forty diagnoses by burden in the year you pick. Area follows Burden (admissions times mean length of stay) and the colour follows Intensity, on a fixed scale so the same shade means the same thing in every year. Switch the year from the dropdown above the chart, e.g. 2020/21 to see the COVID-19 disruption.

3. Temporal heatmap of chapter shares

The treemap shows one year at a time, so it is hard to see how things drift. The heatmap fixes that: each cell is one chapter's share of that year's total burden, with rows ranked by 26-year cumulative share. The 2020/21 column is where Respiratory System and Symptoms & Signs jump while elective-heavy chapters fall back.

4. Strategic quadrant of burden vs intensity

This one is a scatter on log axes: burden on x, intensity on y, one dot per diagnosis in the most recent year. The dashed lines are the medians, so the four quadrants are easy to read. The top-right corner (high volume, high per-patient time) is where length-of-stay reduction would matter most.

5. Stacked stream of super-chapter burden

Here the 21 chapters are rolled up into 10 broader super-chapters and stacked across the 26 years, so total height is total NHS hospital burden. The red guideline marks 2020/21.

6. Sunburst hierarchy

Same hierarchy as the treemap, drawn as a radial: super-chapter in the middle, ICD-10 chapter in the next ring, the diagnosis on the outer ring. Click any slice to zoom in. The colour scale matches the treemap, so the two charts can be read together.

7. What the charts say

Across 26 years the NHS admissions picture is less about one big trend and more about which chapters quietly grow at the expense of others. Digestive System and Musculoskeletal stay the volume backbone; Cancer & Blood and Neurological build per-patient intensity over time; and Respiratory System absorbs the 2020 shock. The treemap alone would have missed that drift, which is why the heatmap and the stream sit alongside it.
